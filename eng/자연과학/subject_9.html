KAIST RESEARCH ACHIEVEMENTS

Development of a
variational method for construction of multi-bump standing waves of
nonlinear Schroedinger equations without a non-degeneracy condition for
their limiting problems

Department of Mathematical Sciences Jaeyoung Byeon

Summary

In many mathematical models the corresponding partial differential equations contain parameters which are big or small. In this case there would be corresponding solutions exhibit some special behavior. Through by certain renormalization, the supposed solutions converge to solutions of simpler limiting partial differential equations. The existence of such kind of solutions depends subtly on the structural stability of the limiting equations. A development of general methodology for construction of such solutions based on the structural stability of the limiting problems is very fundamental and important in the research of partial differential equations. Together with the development of general methodology, various applications of the methodology to related problems will be pursued.

R&D Report

The classical mechanics followed by the ground breaking works of Newton is approximated by quantum mechanics through by Schrodinger with a very small parameter called Plank constant. That kind of description is very common in modern science as we see also in the ground breaking work of Alan Turing on pattern formation. Now partial differential equations with small or large constants are ubiquitous in mathematical sciences. So it is very important to develop general methodology to treat partial differential equations with small or large constants.

There have been many mathematical studies on nonlinear Schrodinger equations which arise from many physical models. Especially, it is known that near any non-minimal critical points of a potential, there are clustering multi-bump semiclassical standing waves of nonlinear Schrodinger equations. The existence of such a kind of solutions was proved by the Lyapunov-Schmidt reduction method when corresponding limiting problems satisfy a non-degeneracy condition. In the research, by developing a totally new variational method, we could prove the existence of clustering multibump semiclassical standing waves of nonlinear Schrodinger equations for optimal class of non-linearities.
